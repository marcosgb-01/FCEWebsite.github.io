Topic: Internet of Things (IoT)

Introduction to IoT

The IoT technology

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the phenomenon that permits objects to process information and connect in between themselves through the internet or other network. Devices collect information with sensors and other perception components and connect to other different devices with little to no human interaction.

Recently, IoT has grown massively in importance. The world has in the last years automated greatly, allowing for intelligent systems in the most quotidian aspects of life.

How is it possible?

IoT was conceptualised a long time ago. However, it is possible thanks to a range of recent technological advancements which include:
Cheaper technological sensors: allowing for companies to produce them more efficiently.
Improved connectivity: Advancements in the internet and network speed.
Cloud platforms development: In recent years cloud infrastructure has experienced a massive improvement.
Analytics development: data infrastructure and A.I.: Machine learning models, big data and data architecture and conversational A.I. have allowed for more user-friendly interfaces and enhanced data-storage and transmission.

IoT applications

With the IoT in our reach, the applications to the modern world are simply infinite. We can collect, use, modify and calculate enormous amounts of data collected that with the help of Big Data Analyst and AI, we can analyze it and apply it from our daily life, healthcare, businesses and to even government applications like city planning and management.

Healthcare

We are aware of the current state of global healthcare. Throughout the pandemic, we were able to see how the lack of previous preparation, management of problems and overall risks faced when dealing with that problem, led to the realization that a change had to be made to our healthcare systems. IoT is starting to revolutionize healthcare. When implementations of healthcare such as wearables are introduced, the increment of data collection will help analysts have more and better-quality information to treat patients.

Smart grids

Smart grids are a network of electricity that uses digital technologies like advanced metering infrastructure (AMI), smart distribution management systems (SDMS), and smart grid communication networks among others to monitor and manage the transportation of electricity from the generation sources to meet the needs of the users.

The usage of smart grids varies from the supply of electricity to consumers using digital communications, and for the better optimization of this process the IoT capabilities are required.

One of the many advantages that IoT brings to smart grids is the automatization of resource usage to minimize power and water consumption of businesses. All of this offers a new horizon in power generation, power delivery, reducing the number of cuts and decreasing the bill costs overall. It also has functionality when using it for deep-learning applications, as IoT brings vast amounts of data, this helps to model and give information with the help of smart grids to sort out high demand responses in peak hour situations.

With the IoT and deep learning implementations we could be able to predict and learn peak hours for high demands of electricity and thus making the processes much more responsive and efficient and make our electricity waste much lower and our efficiency much greater.

Block chains

Block chains are decentralized distributed digital systems consisting of a chain of blocks that contain transactional data shared across a network of individuals that are connected to this network in an encrypted form.

Block chains play a big role regarding IoT ambient, especially when talking about security connections. This is made by a different store of data in chronological order that is inaccessible to adversaries. This impacts several industries like transportation, healthcare or manufacturing by helping to protect IoT systems from cyberattacks and increasing automation through the reduction of the necessity of intermediaries.

Self-driving cars

Self-driving cars have been a big controversy over the years, with high improvements being made constantly, the implementation of IoT could mean the necessary technological advancement to send self-driving cars to a complete reality. With more and more devices being interconnected and sharing large amounts of data, more data traffic is being able to be used to increment the accuracy and the level of trust we have in self-driving cars. With all of this information self-driving cars' future is closer than ever before although security and trustful data is still an emergent issue that needs to be completely solved before being able to improve in this area.

Advantages and disadvantages of IoT

Advantages
Smart grids
A smart grid, through its applications, can, in real time, collect, record and analyze data on the status of the smart components and their performance in order to coordinate the generation, transmission, distribution and consumption of electrical energy. They are capable of monitoring and managing electricity with the aim of improving the efficiency, reliability, sustainability and security of the supply itself.
Process and task automation
While it may seem basic due to the reason for its creation, IoT makes tasks like cleaning a house easier and more efficient, shifting activities that used to be performed entirely by humans to automated solutions. This frees up time for humans to engage in other activities.
Internet connectivity
The cornerstone of this concept is that physical objects can be connected to a network where they previously couldn't, providing access to all their capabilities. This facilitates real-time data transfer and communication between devices and people.
Security
The installation of these systems in certain contexts has helped create a larger security network due to the quick response and predetermined actions in certain situations. Being a real-time system, actions are much more efficient and faster than if a human were to perform them. For example, in a home surveillance system, you can set an alarm to sound when there is movement in the house or specific rooms when you leave home. These anti-theft alarms immediately alert the security company and the police, with the option to view camera footage if needed.
Health
IoT can be implemented in many everyday objects. Several smart devices have been created to monitor various health data such as vital signs, the number of steps taken, and movements. They can even notify medical professionals or emergency contacts when needed, again emphasizing the quick response feature.
Data collection/analysis
Being constantly connected, these objects transmit information to companies, which can use the data for more efficient marketing, offers, or business actions. Moreover, the large volumes of data enable easier pattern recognition and analysis, leading to more effective decision-making.
Development of new technologies
The development of this concept is so vast and potentially beneficial that research in IoT is constantly increasing. This could lead to increased funding for IoT research and, consequently, an increase in Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in the country, enhancing the quality of life.

The future of IoT

Digital twins/Object cloning
This will develop construction and health industries since we will be able to clone materials such as bricks at a larger scale or reproduce body parts such as bones or tissues from the original one so it fits the patients body easily, avoiding complications and reducing rejections from patients.
Artificial Intelligence
Home automation or domotics will get better and we will be able to control more and more aspects of our houses (lights, blinds, doors, kitchen devices, televisions…). AI will also be able to detect illnesses in a patient before they become too dangerous. Intelligent cars will also improve in order to require less human control, helping us to use the time we would be driving, into other things, like working, or caring about our families.
Economy
More complex decentralized networks and money will get digitized and physical money might even disappear in the near future, making it easier to track scammers and other tax offenses in order to protect the users and the civilians that might get involved.
Public infrastructures
They will provide for traffic control, waste management, water management and overall control over the city which will result in a better lifestyle for the average citizen. In manufacturing, IoT will provide a more specialized maintenance method, an improved tracking and monitoring for the project and a more fluid interaction between the worker and the machine.
Communication revolution
Nowadays, some projects like Neuralink, are looking towards a more dynamic connection between human beings without the necessity of a mobile phone or another device, just our brain, for either communicating or retaining information, it might be a big step forward towards a world connected through brains.
House management
Another field in which IoT is getting applied is into our homes, in order to manage the energy fluxes and improve the energetical efficiency by controlling electrical appliances, leverage appliances and more.
Health data
This type of technology makes us reimagine all our daily decisions. Decisions that everyday generate 1 billion GB of data. And can help us track our health levels and change our training to be more efficient as well as change our medical system. Thanks to wearables like smart-watches there are more applications to our data generated into the med-tech sector that is developing. This sector is estimated to revenue almost $87 billion by the end of next year.
Internet
The IoT has a great point of view in order to personalize each interaction with the massive and growing network that is the internet, helping us to interconnect with each other and make our daily life as well as helping us to make decisions faster and more reasonably.

The ethical view

Even though IoT devices can help to make the users life easier, more accessible and interconnected, it may have some negative issues that must be considered in order to make the path of development of these technologies more ethical overall.

The first issue that we might encounter when talking about this theme is the inevitable fact that with the wearables we named before. There exists the risk of being tracked for everything you do, wherever you are doing with whoever you're doing it.

Another issue is connected with the term of security. Due to its small operative system,wearables are more vulnerable than other devices, making it quite more probable to be hacked and get your data breached putting yourself in danger as well as your close friends and family. Ai is getting more advanced being able to modify and create images and photos, this makes users vulnerable to more easily made frauds.

Furthermore, there exists a digital breach, that with more advanced IoT devices, might get bigger with the usage of these technologies, making it impossible for some people to benefit from these devices.

Last but not least, making the amount of IoT devices we mentioned earlier consumes a massive amount of resources and creating more servers might cause an impact on our ecosystem heavily. So, not only we must care about our digital impact but also for our carbon impact.

Conclusion

In our conclusion we must notice the advancements made by this technology over the past few years, thanks to which we can have an easier day to day life. We can also see that the disadvantages have a minor effect and thus are less important than the advantages. As of right now the only main disadvantages for the wide variety and uses of IoT could be separated between the cost and security issues, as they are the main factors that will set this advancement back.

To conclude, we must point that our investigation in this topic makes one realize how much impact IoT has in our lives and how much potential of improvement and growth it has in the near future. We believe that IoT will become one of the most important markets in the next few decades.
